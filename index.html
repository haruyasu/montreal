Geography

Montreal is in the southwest of the province of Quebec. The city covers most of the Island of Montreal at the confluence of the Saint Lawrence and Ottawa Rivers.

Architecture

Many colonial era buildings can be found in Old Montreal with several dating as far back as the late 17th century.

Languages

French is the city's official language and is the language spoken at home by 49.8% of the population of the city, followed by English at 22.8% and 18.3% other languages.

Population

Montreal's population growth has been slow, and its previous 2014 population of 1.75 million is little changed from the 2011 census figure of 1.65 million.

Culture

A cultural heart of classical art and the venue for many summer festivals.

Economy

Montreal has the second-largest economy of Canadian cities based on GDP and the largest in Quebec.

The Climate of Montreal

Summer

Summers are, on the whole, warm and humid with a daily maximum average of 26 to 27 degree in July; temperatures in excess of 30 degree are common.

Winter

Winter brings cold, snowy, windy, and, at times, icy weather, with a daily average ranging from −10.5 to −9 degree in January.
